1. Introduction to Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small text files placed by websites on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit them. These files help the website remember your actions, preferences, and usage patterns to provide you with a better and more personalized experience during subsequent visits. Cookies are widely used across the internet, enabling functionality such as keeping you logged in, remembering language preferences, and compiling analytics data to improve website performance.

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons, pixels, local storage, and device fingerprinting to gather information about your interactions with our website. These technologies collectively help us understand usage trends, optimize features, and offer relevant content or advertising aligned with your interests.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We utilize a variety of cookies, which can be categorized based on their purpose and duration:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly, enabling core navigation, access to secure areas, and user authentication. Without these, some features may not work or the website may not load correctly.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect anonymous information about how visitors use the website, such as pages visited, time spent, and any errors encountered. This data helps us understand user behavior and improve the website's functionality and content.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies remember your choices such as language preferences or location, enhancing your browsing experience by personalizing content and remembering settings between visits.
  • Advertising and Targeting Cookies: These cookies collect information about your browsing habits to deliver relevant ads tailored to your interests. They also help limit the number of times you see an advertisement and measure the effectiveness of ad campaigns.

12. Detailed Explanation of Cookies and Their Functions

Cookies are integral to modern web browsing, enabling websites to recognize returning users, store preferences, and facilitate functionality that enhances the user experience. These tiny data packets are stored on your device by your web browser at the request of websites you visit. Cookies serve a variety of purposes such as authentication, session management, personalization, and analytics.

For example, when you log in to a website, cookies may be used to keep you authenticated so that you donât have to re-enter credentials on every page. Similarly, cookies remember language preferences or region settings, so content is displayed in your preferred format on subsequent visits. Without these, websites would be much less friendly, often requiring repetitive inputs and losing tailoring based on past interactions.

Tracking and analytics cookies allow website owners to gather aggregate information about page visits, user navigation paths, and time spent which helps optimize content layout, performance, and marketing strategies. Advertising cookies enable delivery of targeted ads that are more relevant to user interests by tracking browsing activity across multiple sites.

13. The Difference Between Cookies, Local Storage, and Other Tracking Mechanisms

While cookies are the most common tracking method, other technologies also exist to support website functionality:

  • Local Storage: Browser-based storage that allows data to be saved with no expiration, enabling long-term data retention such as offline app data.
  • Session Storage: Similar to local storage but cleared when the browser tab is closed.
  • Web Beacons and Pixels: Tiny, invisible graphics embedded in webpages or emails that track user engagement and deliver cookies.
  • Device Fingerprinting: Collects device-specific information such as browser version, timezone, plugins, and fonts to uniquely identify a device.

Thrive Wisp Gardening primarily employs cookies for tracking and does not utilize fingerprinting or invasive tracking methods. When we use advanced techniques such as web beacons, they are bundled with cookie usage within our consent management system.
